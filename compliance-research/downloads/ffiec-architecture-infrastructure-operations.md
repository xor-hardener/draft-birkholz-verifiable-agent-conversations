# FFIEC Architecture, Infrastructure, and Operations Handbook

Source: https://ithandbook.ffiec.gov/it-booklets/architecture-infrastructure-and-operations/

## Overview

The FFIEC IT Handbook booklet on "Architecture, Infrastructure, and Operations" addresses "enterprise-wide, process-oriented approaches that relate to the design of technology within the overall business structure, implementation of IT infrastructure components, and delivery of services and value for customers."

## Primary Sections

### Governance (Section II)
Establishes roles for board oversight, senior management, IT leadership (including Chief Architect and Chief Data Officer positions), and operational personnel. The handbook emphasizes policies, standards, procedures, audit processes, and reporting mechanisms.

### Common Risk Topics (Section III)
Covers data governance, IT asset management, environmental representations (network and data flow diagrams), change management, third-party oversight, resilience, remote access, personally-owned devices, and file exchange protocols.

### Architecture (Section IV)
Addresses architecture planning, design objectives, IT architecture design, and enterprise architecture frameworks.

### Infrastructure (Section V)
Details hardware, networks, telecommunications, software (including open-source considerations), and environmental controls (HVAC, fire suppression, water, power, physical access).

### Operations (Section VI)
Includes operational controls, identity/access management, maintenance, configuration management, patch management, backup processes, capacity management, log management, service management, and monitoring/evaluation procedures.

### Evolving Technologies (Section VII)
Examines cloud computing, zero-trust architecture, microservices, artificial intelligence/machine learning, and Internet of Things implementations.

## Relevance to AI Agent Trajectories

Key requirements applicable to AI agent session recording:
1. **Log Management:** Complete audit trails of system operations
2. **Change Management:** Tracking all modifications made by AI systems
3. **Third-Party Oversight:** Accountability for AI vendor systems
4. **Data Governance:** Proper handling of AI-generated content
5. **Monitoring/Evaluation:** Continuous assessment of AI system behavior
